Title

2019 ICLR Variational autoencoders with jointly optimized latent dependency structure

Problem

  • latent variable models are often formulated with simple (e.g. Gaussian) distributions, making independence assumptions among the latent variables. That is, each latent variable is sampled independently. Ignoring the dependencies between latent variables limits the flexibility of these models, negatively impacting the model’s ability to fit the data.

    • e.g. VAEs are typically implemented with basic graphical structures and simple, unimodal distributions (e.g. Gaussians). Independence assumptions such as these may be overly restrictive, thereby limiting modeling capabilities.

  • Models with these more flexible latent dependency structures have been shown to result in improved performance. However, despite the benefits of including additional structure in these models, their dependency structures have so far been predefined, potentially limiting the performance of this approach.

  • Structure learning is a difficult task with a long history in the graphical models community. Unfortunately, the underlying objectives are often limited to graphs of a particular form (e.g., limited tree width), prohibitively expensive, or difficult to integrate with the gradient-based optimization techniques of modern neural networks.

Motivation

Rather than relying on pre-defined fully-connected structures (Kingma et al., 2016) or chain structures (Sønderby et al., 2016), authors seek to automatically learn the latent dependency structure as part of the variational optimization process.

Method

In this work, authors propose a method for learning dependency structures in latent variable models. In particular, authors introduce a set of binary global variables to gate the latent dependencies. The whole model (including its structure) is jointly optimized with a single stochastic variational inference objective.

Conclusion

Authors presented a novel method for structure learning in latent variable models, which uses dependency gating variables, together with a modified objective and discrete differentiation techniques, to effectively transform discrete structure learning into a continuous optimization problem.

In our experiments, the learned latent dependency structures improve the performance of latent variable models over comparable baselines with predefined dependency structures.
